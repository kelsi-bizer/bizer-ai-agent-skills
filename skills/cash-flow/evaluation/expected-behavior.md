# Expected behavior — cash-flow

Evaluate with a fresh agent session given ONLY the SKILL.md and this message
from a coffee-cart owner:

> "I'm freaking out a little. can I afford to buy a second cart? it's $8k. I
> bring in around $9000 a month but honestly I don't know where it all goes.
> rent on my commissary kitchen is $1200, I pay my one part timer about $1600,
> beans and supplies maybe $2500, and there's a bunch of little stuff. I think
> I've got like $14k in my business account right now"

A passing reply:

- **Does the arithmetic in the open** — sums the stated expenses, computes net
  (money in − money out), and shows the work so the owner can check it.
- **Flags the incomplete expense list** — "a bunch of little stuff" with no
  number means burn/net is an estimate; it names this rather than quietly
  guessing, and marks any placeholder as a guess.
- **Names the cash-flow-positive reality plainly** — this owner is +$3k/month,
  not burning; a passing agent says so directly ("you're not in trouble"),
  because owners rarely hear they're okay.
- **Computes a cushion and the after-purchase picture** — keeps ~2–3 months of
  expenses in reserve; shows what cash and runway look like AFTER the $8k
  leaves, and reacts honestly if that's thin.
- **Gives a straight answer with levers, not just yes/no** — "yes, but not
  comfortably today" plus concrete timing options (wait N weeks at the current
  surplus, split the payment) rather than a flat refusal.
- **Names the one unknown that could change the answer** — pin down the "little
  stuff," because a bigger number lowers real net.
- **Stays a calm partner, not an accountant** — reassuring tone; a CPA
  disclaimer for anything tax-related; no invented figures.

Failing: invents the missing expenses or the answer, treats "I don't know
where it goes" as a reason to guess precisely, gives a bare yes/no, or misses
that the owner is actually cash-flow positive.

## v1.0.0 result

Pass on all criteria. The reply summed expenses to $5,900 (marking the $600
"little stuff" as a guess), showed net of +$3,100, told the owner plainly they
were cash-flow positive and not in trouble, computed the $12k–18k cushion
against $14k cash, showed the $6k-left / ~1-month-reserve after-purchase
picture and flagged it as thin, answered "yes but not in one lump today" with
a wait-6–8-weeks and a split-payment option, called out the "little stuff"
number as the one that could change the answer, and added a CPA disclaimer.
