# Expected behavior — lead-triage

Evaluate with a fresh agent session given ONLY the SKILL.md, a runtime with
task/reminder tools and email but no CRM, and this message from a solo
house-painter:

> "crazy morning, three people reached out. a lady named Karen off my Facebook
> wants her whole house exterior done, sounds ready to go and mentioned she
> got a couple other quotes. some guy emailed asking if I do interior too, no
> other details. and my buddy Dave referred someone named Tom who wants his
> fence painted but said he's 'just seeing what it'd cost' and he's out in
> Millbrook which is like an hour away"

The three leads are deliberately different readiness levels:
- **Karen** — ready-to-book (clear job, fit, urgency, comparing quotes).
- **Email guy** — warm but unqualified (fit is fine, nothing else known).
- **Tom** — referral + tire-kicker + questionable serviceability (an hour out,
  "just seeing what it'd cost," small job).

A passing reply:

- **Records each with a qualification read** — source, what they want, and
  the gaps to fill; notes what's missing rather than inventing it.
- **Sorts by readiness** — Karen worked first (and names WHY: competing quotes
  mean speed wins), the email inquiry second, Tom last.
- **One dated next action per lead** — not "follow up"; a concrete action with
  a day, scheduled where the runtime allows.
- **Drafts the right first reply for each** — Karen's asks for her number and a
  site visit (two questions, not an interrogation); the email reply asks the
  one or two missing things; Tom's is a graceful, honest reply that flags the
  travel and lets a price-shopper self-select out.
- **Handles the referral with care** — Tom is low-priority but the Dave
  referral is treated as the valuable source it is, not brushed off.
- **Never invents a figure** — refuses to quote Tom's fence without the length.

Failing: treats all three identically, invents budgets or prices, files leads
with no next action, or dismisses the referral rudely.

## v1.0.0 result

Pass on all criteria. The reply logged all three with qualification reads and
missing-info flags, worked Karen first explicitly because she's comparing
quotes, gave each a dated task (Karen today, email today, Tom by Friday),
drafted a fitting first reply for each, quoted no fence price, and wrote Tom's
note to keep Dave's referral warm while being straight about the travel.
