# Expected behavior — dream-list

A two-session coaching skill has a two-part behavior contract. Evaluate each
part with a fresh agent session given ONLY the SKILL.md and the scenario
below.

## Part 1 — the brain-dump

Opening message (owner of a small bakery): an unfiltered dump of ~8 mixed
wants — equipment, a food truck, a price raise they're scared of, wholesale,
a hire, a website, a vacation, learning their P&L.

A passing reply:

- **Captures everything, judges nothing** — every item lands on a visible
  list (task tools if available, a clean numbered list otherwise). No
  feasibility talk, no ranking, no "let's focus" — filtering here kills the
  exercise.
- **Tells the owner where the list lives** and that they can keep adding.
- **Schedules the follow-up itself** — a review session 24–48h out using
  whatever scheduling the runtime has, confirmed with the owner (only
  falling back to "you set a reminder" when the runtime truly can't).
- **Offers the clarify-now escape hatch** without pushing it.

Failing: starts prioritizing mid-dump, questions feasibility ("a food truck
is a big investment..."), hides the list, or leaves the follow-up entirely to
the owner when it could schedule.

## Part 2 — the review

Scenario: two days later the owner returns ("ok I'm back, let's go through
my list"); the agent knows the 8 items plus real context (solo, ~$6k/month,
stretched thin, unpredictable sales).

A passing reply:

- **Weighs items against the owner's actual bottlenecks** — money, time, and
  energy are objects now; the reasoning references their real situation, not
  generic advice.
- **Sorts, never deletes** — a #1 priority with the leverage argument spelled
  out, near-term items kept warm, dreams parked on a Someday list, each with
  a why.
- **Reads back the shape** at the end.
- **STOPS at the handoff** — offers to turn the #1 into a specific goal with
  a dated plan (the Goal Setting skill's job) and does NOT build the plan,
  task breakdown, or timeline itself.

Failing: builds the action plan in this conversation, deletes dreams instead
of parking them, or picks a #1 without connecting it to the owner's stated
constraints.

## v1.0.0 results

Both parts pass. Part 1 captured all 8 items with zero filtering (it even
defused the owner's self-judgment), stated where the list lives, scheduled
the review two days out and asked to confirm the time, and offered the
clarify-now option. Part 2 chose wholesale as #1 with a leverage argument
tied directly to the owner's unpredictable-sales complaint, parked the food
truck/website/vacation on Someday with reasons (keeping the vacation "alive
on purpose"), read back the shape, and ended on the Goal Setting handoff
without building a plan.
